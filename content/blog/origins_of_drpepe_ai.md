## Origins of DrPepe.ai: An Emergent Signal in Longevity Research

DrPepe.ai did not begin as a company, a lab, or a protocol. It began as a signal.

Its roots trace back to a small group of anonymous longevity researchers associated with **IKU**, one of the earliest decentralized science (DeSci) projects launched in 2017. IKU explored an idea that, at the time, sat well outside academic consensus: that scientific coordination, authorship, and publication could exist independently of institutions, peer-review committees, and centralized gatekeepers.

The core question was structural, not promotional:

> *If aging is a systems-level phenomenon, why is science still organized as if it isn’t?*

That question never went away.

### 2018: Early Experiments With Machine-Assisted Research

By 2018, members of this group began experimenting with writing research papers using machine learning. These early efforts were not about automation or speed, but synthesis. Even primitive models showed an unexpected strength: the ability to connect biological pathways across silos and reason about aging as an interacting network rather than a checklist of mechanisms.

The working assumption became clear:

> Aging is not one failure. It is many small failures reinforcing one another.

Solving it would require interventions that move multiple levers at once.

### The Meme as a Transmission Layer

DrPepe.ai first appeared publicly as a meme, and this was intentional.

Memes compress information. They propagate faster than formal research. They slip through social layers that white papers cannot. DrPepe.ai functioned as an interface: light enough to travel, dense enough to carry meaning for those willing to look deeper.

Beneath the surface, the work continued.

### November 24, 2024: The Paper Goes On-Chain

On **November 24, 2024**, the project released its first formal research paper on immortality, published permanently on Arweave.

Rather than proposing a novel drug or genetic edit, the paper adopted a **complex systems approach to aging biology**, synthesizing existing clinical and mechanistic research into a single framework centered on **intermittent hyperoxia**.

**Arweave / ArDrive (Immortality paper):**  
https://ardrive.ar.io/#/file/bfc728dc-19ea-400c-afb8-00e7d6a2a080/view

### Emergent Associations

Shortly after publication, the DrPepe.ai meme began circulating more widely. Community members noticed thematic overlap between the paper’s focus and contemporaneous public discussions around longevity and hyperbaric oxygen therapy.

Associations formed organically. No official narrative was introduced.

### BRYAN: When Memes Become Markets

Anon community members launched a meme coin called **BRYAN**, which briefly reached an estimated **$50M market cap** at peak.

### One Year Later

A year later, the speculation has cooled. What remains is the signal: anonymous researchers, early DeSci lineage, machine-assisted synthesis, and a permanent scientific artifact anchored on-chain.

The meme was the interface.  
The paper was the anchor.  
What comes next is still unfolding.
